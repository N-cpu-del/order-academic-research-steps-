Vocabulary Tip: Collocations are combinations of words that naturally go together (e.g., "carry out research" instead of "make research").

Model Answer Key

Chronological Steps of Academic Research

1

define the scope

Outline the topic boundary, timelines, and primary questions.

2

get approval

Secure supervisor consent or ethics clearances.

3

do initial research

Gather backgrounds, theories, and existing studies.

4

design a questionnaire/ a survey

Establish standard metrics, queries, or survey outlines.

5

carry out my own research

Execute data collection, distribute questionnaires, or test.

6

analyse the data

Extract insights, evaluate responses, run statistics.

7

summarise the results

Condense figures and main outcomes into clear points.

8

draw conclusions

Discuss implications, limits, and answers to the research question.

9

write up the report

Synthesize everything into the final thesis or paper.
